Guidance

Give as much detail about the claimantâ€™s eating habits generally, including:

  • What sort of food they eat.
  • Do they need prompting to both cook and/or prompting to physically eat food
  • What they eat for breakfast, lunch and dinner.
  • If they are hungry would they eat something?
  • If they are thirsty would they drink something?
  • If low mood is an issue and they state that prompting would be required please indicate how often this does happen and if continuous prompting is needed
  • Whether they make themselves a drink or any snacks throughout the day.
  • Whether they can use standard cutlery or have any adapted cutlery.
  • If a therapeutic source, whether they can prepare this themselves
  • Ensure all aspects of safety and supervision have been considered
  • Brief description of key restriction e.g. needs prompting due to low mood or will snack on crisps and biscuits but needs prompting to make a meal.
  • Critical analysis of evidence e.g. showed stable mood, discharged from the specialist and has adequate motivation to care for children.
  • Review of inconsistencies and consider the usual features/progress/nature of the condition.
  • Consider safety.
  • Consider whether there is a clinical/functional need for aids to be used or whether they are being used for hazard aversion. (if aids have been discounted give full explanation).
  • Where applicable, include details of the nature of any prompting/supervision and why it is required. (If prompting/supervision is claimed and being discounted fully evidence reasons why).
  • Summarise and refer to the evidence gathered.
