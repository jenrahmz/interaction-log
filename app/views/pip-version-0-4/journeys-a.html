Guidance

Probing questions are needed to establish whether they can travel to unfamiliar places as well as familiar ones and whether they can re-organise a journey if their route is disturbed. It should also be established whether the claimant needs to be accompanied rather than choosing to have a companion. Where the claimant suffers overwhelming psychological distress, it meets the high threshold.

Please give an indication of the following:

  • Whether and how often they go out and whether this is ever alone or always accompanied and whether this is due to the risk of having a seizure or needs accompanying due to the risk of a seizure.
  • Whether they are able to attend doctors/hospital appointments, go shopping, walk a dog or undertake family/friend social activities etc. Is this is ever alone or always accompanied?
  • An indication as to what ‘psychological distress’ looks like for the claimant, including a description of their symptoms and what happens to them.
  • Whether there are any limitations to abilities they have with familiar as opposed to unfamiliar journeys.
  • Hearing – is the claimant able to hear a tannoy system – e.g. at a train station reporting a change of platform
  • If they have visual problems indicate whether they use an orientation cane or guide dog for every journey.
  • What type of orientation aid white stick is used
  • Can they safely orientate e.g. see kerbs, identify changes in terrain, road crossings and such. How they would respond if their journey was disrupted or diverted.
  • How they would respond if lost.
  • If they have cognitive problems an indication as to whether they are able to travel alone to familiar places.
  • Whether memory has an impact.
  • Does the claimant experience loss or altered state of consciousness, if so, when, how long, how does this impact on their safety following a route?
  • In cases of profoundly deaf clearly explain how they deal with using public transport e.g. change of platform in station
  • An indication as to whether they can undertake a journey if they have to
  • Critical analysis of evidence e.g. Variability shows that seizures occur on average 3 times a month, HOC states they have no aura, drop to the floor and convulse lasting 2 minutes, FE confirms this and now under care of specialist nurse.
  • Review inconsistencies; consider usual features/progress/nature of the condition.
  • Summarise and refer to the evidence gathered.
