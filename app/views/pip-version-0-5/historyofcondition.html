Guidance

The information in this section is a prompt to what Case Managers require. It is not a prescriptive tick list and there are no mandatory questions.

The bullets here should be about the history of conditions, symptoms and reviews.

If this is an Award Review please indicate that this is since the last PA3/4 was completed.

Please list ALL the conditions the claimant has listed on their PIP2, or any functionally relevant conditions not listed on the PIP2 but mentioned at the assessment, including:

  • Onset of current symptoms: always include the date (or approximate time period) of the on-set of the current level of functional impairment.
  • Initial symptoms
  • Diagnosis: who made it and how.
  • Details of any associated symptoms and how often are they affected by those symptoms.
  • Who monitors them for that condition, when they last saw them and how regularly they see them.
  • Details of all on-going treatment or therapy, who provides it and how often it occurs.
  • Whether there is anything that exacerbates or eases the condition/symptoms (e.g. stress).
  • Whether they have ever been hospitalised for the condition, including dates if known.
  • Why a treatment has stopped or been withdrawn.
  • Details of any treatment/surgery the claimant is waiting for, including information of why claimant has been referred for it.
  • Additional Questions if relevant (suicide, self-harm, drugs and alcohol)

In learning difficulty/learning disability cases consider instead:

  • When was diagnosis / what was the level of diagnosis / by whom
  • Schooling history (statement of educational needs â€“ key FE, mainstream or special, extra support, adaptations)
  • Qualifications (exams/further education/life skills college/supported college)
  • Have they ever worked? (doing what, what support, what level of independence and responsibility)
  • What do they find difficult now/need support with/any additional symptoms or comorbidities.
  • Intervention (GP, social worker, support worker, care support, housing support, appointee etc)
  • Additional questions (self-harm, suicide, alcohol and recreational drugs).
  • Consider vulnerability, insight and safeguarding.
